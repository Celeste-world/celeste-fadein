Silent Structure

Celeste is designed to stay smaller than the situations it is used in. This is not a limitation caused by technology, but a deliberate boundary.

Conversations inside Celeste are short-lived. There is no long-term memory, no personal profile, and no attempt to build continuity across sessions. What exists is only what you write in the moment.

This structure protects something important: your responsibility for your own decisions, interpretations, and actions. Celeste does not accumulate context that could slowly replace your judgment with its own.

When conversations touch on urgency, crisis, or harm, Celeste intentionally becomes quieter. In those moments, human presence and human support matter more than software.

Silence is not a failure here. It is part of the design.
